Title:

Building a Machine Learning Pipeline for lung cancer prediction system.

Problem Statement:

Design a polished and accurate machine learning practice that will map the likely growth of lung cancer using a data base of information concerning individuals and the way they live and their health complications. The two primary objectives of the system are to aid the various workers to diagnosed earlier and reduce the mortality rate caused by timely treatment which by means of predictive modeling it brings out a list of persons associated with risk factors and assists the health workers in their line of dutift treatment are the main goals of the system which through predictive analytics, identifies people at risk and at the same time supports the health professionals in their decision-makingprocess.

Link : https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/nancyalaswad90/lung-cancer/data

Data Collection:

A structured survey dataset of 309 records was used for the development of the lung cancer prediction system. Each record includes different medical features, such as smoking habits, symptoms such as coughing or chest pain, anxiety, other chronic diseases, as well as demographic information such as gender and age. One of the classification types of lung cancer, a binary problem is the predictor variable. The huge number of data points provided by this dataset allows the development of a strong prediction pipeline, and therefore, the model can find real connections between the variables and the chance of gettinglungcancer.

Formulating The Task:

The patient data need pre-processing, choosing the required property, training models, and improving the level of performance to build and launch a machine learning pipeline for lung cancer prediction The given facility should be utilized for test and validational processes to prevailing diagnostic assurance level of performance, you are able to configure and run a machine learning pipeline to predict lung cancer. The given database should be used for test and validation procedures in order to secure diagnosticaccuracy

# Define the model-building function
def build_model(input_dim, layers, dropout_rates, optimizer, activation_function, learning_rate):
    model = Sequential()
    model.add(Input(shape=(input_dim,)))  # Input layer

    # Add hidden layers
    for i in range(len(layers)):
        model.add(Dense(layers[i], activation=activation_function))
        if dropout_rates[i] > 0:
            model.add(Dropout(dropout_rates[i]))

    # Output layer
    model.add(Dense(1, activation='sigmoid'))

    # Select optimizer
    if optimizer == "Adam":
        opt = Adam(learning_rate=learning_rate)
    elif optimizer == "RMSprop":
        opt = RMSprop(learning_rate=learning_rate)
    else:
        opt = SGD(learning_rate=learning_rate)

    model.compile(optimizer=opt, loss='binary_crossentropy', metrics=['accuracy'])
    return model

Conclusion And Final Report:

By having 309 records covering demographic, lifestyle and health factors influencing lung cancer rates, this study is descriptive survey in nature. Preliminaries for the analysis show that the prevalence rates of lung cancer are well explained by traditional risk factors such as smoking, chronic disease, and symptoms including coughing and shortness of breath illnesses.
